Add a heat network - introduction

{{title}}

Your heat network is a 'district network'. This means it supplies heat to more than one building.

There are a number of common configurations for this type of heat network. This is how we need you to provide information for each of them.

District heat network with single premises buildings

This district network has its own energy centre and connects to 5 buildings. Each building is a single premises.

What to submit

We expect your submission for this network to include information about all 5 buildings and the energy centre.

District heat network with 'connected communal networks'

This district network has its own energy centre and connects to 3 single premises buildings, as well as 2 blocks of flats.

We consider the blocks of flats to be communal networks in their own right (what we call 'connected communal networks'). Do not include them in this submission.

What to submit

For this district network, we expect you to register these 3 heat networks separately.

District heat network comprising energy centre and distribution pipework

This district network has its own energy centre and connects to a plot of 3 houses that is operated by another entity.

What to submit

We expect your submission for this network to include the energy centre only.

District heat network that only connects to communal networks

Alternatively, this district network connects to 2 communal networks that you operate.

What to submit

For this district network, we expect the following 3 heat network registrations.

We expect your submission for the district network to include the energy centre only.

We expect your submission for this network to include the energy centre only.

District heat network that does not have its own energy centre

This district network is a plot of 3 houses that gets its energy from an energy centre in another heat network.

What to submit

We expect your submission for this network to include the 3 houses only.
